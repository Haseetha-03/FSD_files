Mastitis (Cow)

Confidence: 95%

Mastitis is an inflammation of the mammary gland and udder tissue, commonly caused by bacterial infection, leading to reduced milk quality and production.

Animal Health Management & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Consult a veterinarian for antibiotic treatment.
  • Frequent milking of affected quarter to flush out infection.
  • Apply anti-inflammatory drugs as prescribed.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Implement proper milking hygiene (pre-dip, post-dip).
  • Maintain clean and dry bedding for animals.
  • Regular herd health checks and culling of chronic cases.
  • Ensure proper nutrition to boost immunity.

For effective mastitis control, a comprehensive farm management plan is essential. Consult your vet regularly.

Mold Contamination (Silage)

Confidence: 88%

Mold contamination in silage indicates poor fermentation or exposure to air, leading to reduced nutritional value and potential mycotoxin production harmful to animals.

Fodder/Feed Management & Prevention

Control Measures:

  • Discard visibly moldy portions of feed.
  • Improve storage conditions to prevent moisture and air exposure.
  • Consider using mold inhibitors if appropriate.

Preventive Strategies:

  • Ensure proper ensiling techniques for silage.
  • Store dry feed in cool, dry, and well-ventilated areas.
  • Regularly clean feed bunks and storage areas.

Regular feed analysis and proper storage are crucial for maintaining feed quality and animal health.

Nutrition Recommendation

Recommended Nutrition Plan:

For lactating cows, a diet with 18% crude protein and 70% TDN (Total Digestible Nutrients) is recommended. Ensure adequate calcium, phosphorus, and trace minerals through supplements or mineral blocks.

Always consult a veterinary nutritionist for precise feed formulations based on animal specific needs and local feed availability.
