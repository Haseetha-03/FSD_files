Varroa Mite Infestation

Confidence: 95%

Varroa destructor mites are external parasites that attack and feed on honey bees, weakening colonies and transmitting viruses.

Bee Health Management & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Apply approved miticides (e.g., formic acid, oxalic acid) as per instructions.
  • Powdered sugar dusting can dislodge mites temporarily.
  • Consider drone brood removal if infestation is severe.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Regular monitoring of mite levels (e.g., sugar roll, alcohol wash).
  • Introduce Varroa-resistant bee genetics if available.
  • Maintain strong, healthy colonies to naturally resist pests.
  • Practice integrated pest management (IPM) strategies.

Consistent monitoring and timely treatment are crucial for managing Varroa mites and ensuring colony health.

American Foulbrood (AFB)

Confidence: 90%

American Foulbrood is a highly destructive bacterial disease of honey bee larvae, leading to colony collapse. Infected brood appears sunken and discolored.

Hive/Comb Management & Prevention

Control Measures:

  • Burn severely infected hives and equipment.
  • Re-queen affected colonies with resistant stock.
  • Administer antibiotics (e.g., oxytetracycline) under veterinary guidance.

Preventive Strategies:

  • Inspect colonies regularly for signs of disease.
  • Practice good apiary hygiene and sanitation.
  • Avoid feeding honey or pollen from unknown sources.
  • Isolate and quarantine new colonies before introducing to existing apiary.

Early detection and strict biosecurity are vital for preventing the spread of American Foulbrood.

Forage/Nutrition Recommendation

Recommended Forage/Nutrition Plan:

For Apis Mellifera during spring build-up with medium nectar and low pollen availability, supplement with 1:1 sugar syrup and protein patties to stimulate brood rearing and colony growth.

Monitor colony stores and adjust feeding based on local floral conditions.

Nosema Disease

Microsporidian parasite affecting adult bee gut, leading to dysentery and reduced lifespan.

Sacbrood Virus

Viral disease of honey bee brood, causing larvae to die and appear sac-like.

Small Hive Beetle

Pest that infests hives, consuming honey and pollen, and sliming combs.

Wax Moth Infestation

Larvae tunnel through comb, destroying beeswax and weakening colonies.

Chalkbrood Disease

Fungal disease affecting bee larvae, turning them into hard, chalk-like mummies.

Colony Collapse Disorder (CCD)

Phenomenon where worker bees disappear from a hive, leaving queen, brood, and food.
