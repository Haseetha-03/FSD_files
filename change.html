Varroa Mite Infestation

Confidence: 85%

Varroa mites are external parasites that attack honey bees, weakening colonies and transmitting viruses. Early detection is crucial for effective management.

Hive Health Management & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Apply approved miticides (e.g., oxalic acid, formic acid) as per guidelines.
  • Powdered sugar dusting to dislodge mites.
  • Consider drone brood removal.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Regular hive inspections for mite levels.
  • Use screened bottom boards.
  • Breed for Varroa-resistant bees.
  • Maintain strong, healthy colonies.

For severe infestations or specific mite types, consult a local beekeeping association or apiary expert.

Wax Moth Larvae

Confidence: 90%

Wax moths are common pests that damage honeycomb and stored bee equipment, especially in weak colonies or stored frames.

Pest/Predator Management

Control Measures:

  • Freeze infested combs for 24-48 hours to kill larvae and eggs.
  • Maintain strong colonies that can defend against pests.
  • Use moth traps or Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) for severe cases.

Preventive Strategies:

  • Store combs properly in airtight containers or well-ventilated areas.
  • Ensure hives are healthy and robust.
  • Keep apiary clean and free of debris.

Implement integrated pest management (IPM) strategies and consult experienced beekeepers for persistent issues.
