Treatment & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Treat with an appropriate antibiotic or disinfectant as per veterinary advice.
  • Increase aeration and water exchange to improve oxygen levels.
  • Isolate affected fish if possible to prevent the spread of the disease.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Monitor water quality parameters (ammonia, nitrite, pH) regularly.
  • Maintain proper stocking density to reduce stress.
  • Ensure biosecurity measures are in place to prevent pathogen introduction.
  • Provide a high-quality, balanced diet to support the fish's immune system.

For severe or persistent health issues, consult with an aquatic veterinarian.

Explore Aquaculture Database / Common Issues

Low Oxygen Levels (Hypoxia)

Insufficient dissolved oxygen in the water, causing fish to gasp at the surface and become lethargic.

Ammonia Toxicity

High levels of ammonia due to waste buildup, leading to tissue damage and death if untreated.

Fungal Infection (Saprolegnia)

A common fungus appearing as white or gray cotton-like growths on the body, fins, or eyes of fish.

Stress & Crowding

Overstocking or poor handling can lead to stress, weakening immune systems and causing disease.

Viral Outbreaks

Highly contagious viral diseases that can cause mass mortality and devastate entire populations.
