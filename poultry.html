Avian Influenza (Bird)

Confidence: 92%

Avian Influenza (Bird Flu) is a highly contagious viral disease affecting poultry, causing respiratory distress, reduced egg production, and high mortality.

Bird Health Management & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Isolate sick birds immediately to prevent spread.
  • Administer antiviral medication if available and prescribed by a vet.
  • Improve ventilation and sanitation in infected areas.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Implement strict biosecurity measures (e.g., footbaths, restricted access).
  • Vaccinate flocks against common poultry diseases as per local guidelines.
  • Control wild bird access to poultry areas to minimize disease transmission.
  • Regularly clean and disinfect poultry houses and equipment.

For effective disease control, implement a comprehensive biosecurity plan and consult a poultry veterinarian for diagnosis and treatment.

Aflatoxin Contamination (Feed)

Confidence: 90%

Aflatoxins are toxic compounds produced by certain molds in feed, causing liver damage, immunosuppression, and reduced productivity in poultry.

Feed/Water Quality Management & Prevention

Control Measures:

  • Discard visibly moldy or contaminated feed.
  • Provide fresh, clean drinking water to birds daily.
  • Consider using mold inhibitors in feed if necessary.

Preventive Strategies:

  • Store feed in dry, cool, and well-ventilated areas away from pests.
  • Prevent rodent and insect access to feed storage areas.
  • Regularly clean and disinfect water lines and drinkers to prevent biofilm growth.

Regular testing of feed and water, along with proper storage and hygiene, are critical for poultry health and productivity.

Nutrition Recommendation

Recommended Nutrition Plan:

For broiler starter chicks, a feed with 20-23% crude protein and 2800-3000 Kcal/kg ME is recommended. Ensure adequate vitamins (A, D3, B complex) and minerals (Calcium, Phosphorus) for optimal growth.

Always consult a poultry nutritionist for precise feed formulations based on bird specific needs and local feed availability.
