White Spot Syndrome Virus (WSSV) is a highly virulent disease in shrimp, causing rapid mortality. Strict biosecurity measures are essential.

Aquatic Health Management & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Isolate affected animals and remove dead stock promptly.
  • Improve water quality parameters (DO, pH, ammonia).
  • Consult an aquatic veterinarian for specific medication.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Implement strict biosecurity protocols for new stock.
  • Regularly monitor and maintain optimal water quality.
  • Use disease-resistant strains and balanced nutrition.
  • Avoid overcrowding in ponds/tanks.

For severe outbreaks or specific diseases, consult a local aquaculture expert or diagnostic laboratory.

Avian Predators Detected

Confidence: 88%

Birds like kingfishers and cormorants can cause significant losses in open ponds. Protective measures are recommended.

Pest/Predator Management

Control Measures:

  • Install bird netting over ponds to deter avian predators.
  • Use scarecrows or reflective deterrents.
  • For snakes/mammals, secure fencing around the farm.

Preventive Strategies:

  • Maintain optimal water levels to deter wading birds.
  • Clear vegetation around ponds that can harbor pests.
  • Implement biological control for aquatic insect pests.

Implement integrated pest management (IPM) strategies and consult local aquaculture experts for persistent issues.
