Varroa mites are a serious parasite that feeds on adult honey bees and developing brood, weakening the colony and transmitting viruses. An infestation can lead to significant colony decline and death if left untreated. Regular monitoring and integrated pest management are crucial.

Treatment & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Use a targeted miticide approved for your region, following all label instructions.
  • Consider organic treatments like oxalic acid or thymol for a more natural approach.
  • Monitor mite drop levels before and after treatment to assess effectiveness.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Introduce mite-resistant queen bees and genetics into your apiary.
  • Practice drone brood removal as a non-chemical control method.
  • Perform regular monthly inspections to catch infestations early.
  • Maintain good sanitation and strong, healthy colonies.

For severe infestations or complex issues, consult with a professional beekeeper or state apiculturist immediately.
