Udder Health Result

Mastitis Detected

Confidence: 90%

Mastitis is an inflammation of the udder, typically caused by bacterial infection. It leads to reduced milk yield and poor quality. It's identified by a swollen, hot udder and often causes clots or flakes in the milk. Immediate veterinary attention is required.

Treatment & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Administer prescribed intramammary antibiotics.
  • Strip the affected quarter multiple times daily to flush out bacteria.
  • Consult a veterinarian for a definitive diagnosis and treatment plan.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Practice strict milking hygiene, including pre- and post-milking teat dips.
  • Maintain a clean, dry environment in the barn and stalls.
  • Ensure proper milking machine function and sanitation.
  • Regularly monitor Somatic Cell Count (SCC) to detect subclinical mastitis early.

For severe or persistent herd health issues, consult with a certified large animal veterinarian.
