Coccidiosis Detected

Confidence: 85%

Coccidiosis is a parasitic disease caused by protozoa that infect the intestines of poultry. Symptoms include watery or bloody droppings, ruffled feathers, and poor growth. It is a common and serious threat to flock health. Immediate treatment and hygiene improvements are essential.

Treatment & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Administer a coccidiostat medication as prescribed by a vet.
  • Provide electrolytes and vitamins in drinking water to support recovery.
  • Isolate severely affected birds to prevent further spread.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Maintain a clean, dry coop and change litter regularly.
  • Use medicated feed or vaccinate chicks against Coccidiosis.
  • Ensure proper ventilation to reduce moisture and ammonia buildup.
  • Implement a strict biosecurity protocol to prevent disease introduction.

For severe or widespread flock health issues, consult with a certified poultry veterinarian immediately.
