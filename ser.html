Silkworm Health Analysis Result

Pebrine Disease

Confidence: 92%

Pebrine is a highly contagious protozoan disease affecting silkworms, characterized by black spots on the body and irregular growth.

Silkworm Health Management & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Isolate and destroy infected silkworms and leaves.
  • Disinfect rearing trays and equipment thoroughly.
  • Maintain strict hygiene in the rearing house.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Use disease-free layings (eggs) from certified sources.
  • Practice proper disinfection of rearing houses before each batch.
  • Control temperature and humidity within optimal ranges.
  • Ensure good ventilation and avoid overcrowding.

Regular monitoring and adherence to biosecurity protocols are crucial for preventing silkworm diseases.

Mulberry Leaf Health Analysis Result

Mulberry Powdery Mildew

Confidence: 88%

Mulberry powdery mildew is a fungal disease causing white, powdery patches on leaves, reducing their nutritional value for silkworms.

Mulberry Leaf Management & Prevention

Control Measures:

  • Prune and destroy infected leaves and branches.
  • Apply appropriate fungicides (e.g., wettable sulfur) as per recommendations.
  • Improve air circulation within the plantation.

Preventive Strategies:

  • Plant resistant mulberry varieties.
  • Maintain proper spacing and regular pruning.
  • Ensure balanced fertilization to promote healthy growth.

Regular inspection and proper agronomic practices are key to healthy mulberry leaves for silkworms.

Mulberry Nutrition Recommendation

Recommended Mulberry Nutrition:

For loamy soil with a pH of 6.5, a balanced NPK fertilizer (e.g., 100:50:50 kg/ha) is recommended for mulberry. Supplement with organic manure for improved leaf quality.

Always perform a soil test for precise recommendations.
